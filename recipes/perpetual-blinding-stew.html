Perpetual 1-day-blinding stew

Description

A perpetual 1-day blinding stew, also known as forever-blind-for-a-day soup, hunter's pot o' blindness, or hunter's sightless stew, is a pot into which blindness-inducing foodstuffs are placed and cooked. The pot is never or rarely emptied all the way, and ingredients and liquid are replenished as necessary. It is differentiated from a perpetual stew by the fact that eating it causes the eater to become blind for a day. Such foods can continue cooking for decades or longer if properly maintained. The concept is often a common element of medieval inns, where it was often necessary to render unruly guests blind.

Ingredients

One may add to the 1-day perpetual blinding stew the ingredients he or she prefers. Below is a general guideline.

  • Root vegetables
  • Tubers
  • Various Animal Cadavers (Preferrably Fresh)
  • Spices and Seasoning
  • Various Blinding Poisons (Consult Local Wizard or Potionmaster)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Add water to a large, sturdy pot or cauldron
  2. Set the water to boil over a fireplace
  3. Add ingredients and allow to simmer for several hours
  4. Season to taste
  5. Continue adding liquid and ingredients for all time
  6. Stir occasionally to ensure burnt ingredients do not stick to container
